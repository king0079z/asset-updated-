Introduction & Purpose

This Service Level Agreement ("SLA") defines the performance standards, support obligations, and service commitments that AssetXAI (the "Vendor") provides to the Qatar Red Crescent Society (QRCS) (the "Client") in connection with the AssetXAI Enterprise Asset Management, Ticketing, and RFID Platform deployed across QRCS operational sites.

This agreement establishes measurable benchmarks for system availability, incident response, resolution timelines, escalation procedures, and reporting — ensuring QRCS receives a consistent, reliable, and high-quality service experience throughout the contract term.

Commitment: AssetXAI is committed to delivering enterprise-grade support that maximises system uptime, minimises operational disruption, and continuously improves service quality for all 920 QRCS users across 7 sites and 30,000 tracked assets.

Planned Maintenance Windows

Scheduled maintenance is performed outside peak operational hours to minimise impact on QRCS operations:

  • Standard Window: Fridays and Saturdays, 02:00 – 06:00 Arabia Standard Time (AST)
  • Notice Period: Minimum 72 hours advance notice via email and in-platform notification
  • Emergency Patching: Critical security patches deployed within 24 hours with 2-hour advance notice where operationally feasible
  • Maximum Planned Downtime: 4 hours per month, not counted against uptime SLA
Availability Calculation: Monthly Uptime % = ((Total Minutes − Unplanned Downtime Minutes) ÷ Total Minutes) × 100. Planned maintenance windows and third-party infrastructure incidents are excluded from this calculation.

Uptime Monitoring

AssetXAI operates continuous automated monitoring of all platform components. Synthetic health checks run every 60 seconds from multiple geographic nodes. The AssetXAI status page provides real-time transparency for QRCS administrators.

Incident Classification & Priority Levels

All support requests are classified into five priority levels. Classification is performed by the AssetXAI support team upon ticket receipt, taking into account business impact, number of users affected, and operational urgency.

Priority Classification Definition Examples
P1 · Critical System-Wide Outage Complete platform unavailability or data loss affecting all users. No workaround available. Platform inaccessible; database failure; all RFID readers offline; authentication failure
P2 · High Major Functionality Loss Core functionality severely impaired affecting multiple users or a critical site. Partial workaround exists. Ticket creation failed; RFID zone alerts not triggering; bulk asset import failing; SLA engine stopped
P3 · Medium Partial Degradation Non-critical feature impaired or single-user issue. Workaround available. Business continues. Report export error; dashboard widget not loading; notification email delayed; filter not working
P4 · Low Minor Issue / Query Minor cosmetic issue, question, or enhancement request with no operational impact. UI misalignment; translation request; how-to question; configuration guidance
P5 · Enhancement Feature Request New feature or improvement suggestion. Logged for product roadmap consideration. New report format; additional dashboard widget; custom field request; integration extension
Client Right to Reclassify: QRCS may request reclassification of any ticket if they believe the initial priority does not reflect operational impact. Reclassification requests are reviewed within 4 business hours during standard support hours and the ticket priority is updated accordingly with QRCS confirmation.

Clock Pause Conditions

Resolution clocks are paused in the following circumstances and resume immediately upon condition clearance. These pauses protect both parties and ensure that resolution time reflects only active engineering effort:

  • Awaiting information, credentials, access, or approvals from QRCS required to diagnose or resolve the issue
  • Issue rooted in third-party systems (Microsoft Dynamics 365, Azure AD, RFID hardware vendor, Supabase, Vercel) and pending their resolution
  • QRCS has accepted a workaround and requested deferral of the permanent fix to a scheduled maintenance window
  • Scheduled maintenance windows affecting the relevant platform component
  • Pending QRCS user acceptance testing (UAT) for a delivered fix prior to closure
  • Ticket placed on hold at QRCS request pending internal decision or budget approval
  • Resolution requires a major platform release, schema migration, or third-party library update that must be batched into the next release cycle
  • The issue cannot be reproduced in a non-QRCS environment and requires access to QRCS live data — pending data provision or anonymised sample from QRCS
Complex Investigations: For technically complex incidents requiring deep root-cause analysis, code archaeology, or third-party vendor co-ordination, AssetXAI may request a formal extension of the resolution timeline. Extensions require written agreement from the QRCS designated IT contact and will be clearly documented in the ticket history with a revised target date.

QRCS Responsibilities

To enable AssetXAI to deliver services within the commitments set out in this SLA, QRCS agrees to the following responsibilities:

  • Designated IT Contact: Maintain at least one designated QRCS IT contact who is the primary liaison for all support and change requests
  • Timely Information: Respond to AssetXAI requests for information, access, or approvals within 1 business day to avoid resolution clock pauses
  • Accurate Reporting: Report incidents through the designated support channels with accurate description, priority assessment, and impact details
  • User Training: Ensure all 920 QRCS users complete the AssetXAI onboarding training provided at project go-live
  • Infrastructure: Maintain adequate network connectivity, device compatibility, and Azure AD configuration as agreed during deployment
  • Access Provision: Grant AssetXAI engineers timely access to QRCS-side systems (Azure AD, network logs) required for diagnosis of complex issues
  • Change Approval: Review and approve or reject proposed major changes within the agreed notice periods
  • Security: Safeguard API keys, admin credentials, and RFID webhook secrets. Report any suspected security breach immediately

Breach Notification

In the event of a confirmed data security breach affecting QRCS data, AssetXAI will notify the QRCS designated contact within 24 hours of detection, with a full incident report and remediation plan within 72 hours.

  • All QRCS data is logically isolated from other tenants within the multi-tenant architecture
  • AssetXAI staff access to QRCS production data requires QRCS approval and is fully audited
  • Penetration testing is conducted annually; results summary shared with QRCS upon request
